# Building a Second Brain — CODE and PARA Summary

The **Building a Second Brain** (BASB) methodology, developed by Tiago Forte, is a popular personal knowledge management (PKM) system. It helps people **capture, organize, distill, and use** the vast amount of information they encounter in daily life—turning it into an external, digital extension of your mind to reduce stress, boost creativity, and produce better output.

The core of the system revolves around a few foundational frameworks and principles.

### The CODE Framework (The Main Operational Process)
This is the **central four-step workflow** for building and using a Second Brain. It's how you consistently turn consumed information into creative results and action.

- **Capture** — Quickly save ideas, notes, quotes, articles, insights, and anything potentially valuable before it disappears from your mind. **The goal is to relieve your biological brain of storage duties** (your brain is for having ideas, not holding them).
- **Organize** — Put captured items into a structure where they'll be useful later, **primarily by actionability rather than rigid categories**.
- **Distill** — Refine and summarize notes over time to highlight the most valuable essence (often using techniques like **Progressive Summarization**).
- **Express** — Turn your refined knowledge into real-world output: writing, speaking, creating projects, sharing, teaching, building products, etc.

**CODE is the recurring cycle that keeps your Second Brain alive and productive.**

### The PARA Method (The Core Organization System)
This is the most widely recommended way to **organize** notes and files in a Second Brain. It sorts everything by **how actionable it is right now**.

- **Projects** — Time-bound efforts with a specific goal or deadline (e.g., "Launch website redesign", "Plan family vacation 2026").
- **Areas** — Ongoing responsibilities or standards to maintain (e.g., "Health & Fitness", "Finances", "Professional Development", "Home Maintenance").
- **Resources** — Topics of interest or general reference material that don't have immediate action attached (e.g., "Machine Learning", "Productivity Techniques", "Recipes", "Design Inspiration").
- **Archives** — Inactive items from the above categories (completed projects, outdated areas/resources moved here for decluttering but easy retrieval).

**PARA works across any digital tool** (Notion, Obsidian, Evernote, Roam, etc.) and is designed to be simple, universal, and flexible.

### The 10 Core Principles
Tiago Forte has also outlined these **10 guiding principles** that underpin the entire BASB philosophy (drawn from his teaching and writing):

1. **Borrowed Creativity** — Creativity comes from remixing and building on others' ideas (stand on the shoulders of giants).
2. **The Capture Habit** — Reliably save thoughts and inspirations the moment they appear—don't rely on memory.
3. **Idea Recycling** — Reuse and repurpose the same ideas multiple times across projects instead of always starting from scratch.
4. **Projects Over Categories** — **Organize primarily around active projects (not abstract topics), because action creates momentum.**
5. **Slow Burns** — Let ideas develop gradually over time rather than forcing instant breakthroughs.
6. **Start with Abundance** — Collect far more material than you think you'll need—quantity leads to quality later.
7. **Resurface** — **Bring old notes back into view periodically** so forgotten ideas can spark new connections.
8. **Actionable Notes** — Make notes future-useful by including your own thoughts, connections, and potential actions.
9. **Progressive Improvement** — Refine notes iteratively over time (don't aim for perfection on first pass).
10. **Share What You Know** — Expressing knowledge (teaching, writing, building) is the ultimate way to solidify and multiply its value.

Together, these elements form a **lightweight yet powerful system** for thriving in an age of information overload. Many people start simply by **adopting CODE + PARA in their favorite note-taking app**.

If you're just beginning, the most common quick-start advice is: **pick one app, set up PARA folders/areas, start capturing everything interesting without overthinking organization, and periodically distill your best notes.** Over time, the system compounds.
